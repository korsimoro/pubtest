Main Parties

Issuers. Issue verifiable claims to holders. E.g., DMV says you're a driver, UN says you're a refugee.

Holder. Person who holds credentials.

Repository. Where a Holder stores a verifiable claim. Cannot lock Holder in. (Container) The only person who interacts with Repository (for now) is the Holder!

Inspector. Look at credential to verify it for validity. May be Issuer.

The Tech

DID Registry. Allocates identifiers. An assignment authority to ensure uniqueness. Issuer, Holder, and Inspector work with it to issue and verify DID. (DID = Decentralized Identifier.)

Public Ledgers. Some credentials may be issued into public ledgers. For example, information ona debarred doctor. This is a controversial part of the model.

Optional Parties

Relying Party. Party who delegates inspection to an Inspector because they don't have competency themselves. (Bar is Relying Party, Bouncer is Inspector)

Witness. Another form of subsidiary issuer.
